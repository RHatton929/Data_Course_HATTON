Modeling:

Now, Ill define a few models with the 4 variables in the data set:

names(df)
## [1] "admit" "gre"   "gpa"   "rank"
mod1 <- glm(data = df, 
            formula = admit ~ gre + gpa,
            family = "binomial")

mod2 <- glm(data = df,
            formula = admit ~ gre * gpa,
            family = "binomial")

mod3 <- glm(data = df, 
            formula = admit ~ gre + gpa + rank,
            family = "binomial")

mod4 <- glm(data = df,
            formula = admit ~ gre * gpa + rank,
            family = "binomial")

mod5 <- glm(data = df,
            formula = admit ~ gre * gpa * rank,
            family = "binomial")

While theres not a whole lot of ways to make a model with 4 total variables overly complex, Im going to use stepwise AIC to make one final model:

step <- stepAIC(mod5)

I can then view the formula and assign it to my model:

step$formula
## admit ~ gre + gpa + rank + gre:gpa
mod6 <- glm(data = df,
          formula = step$formula,
          family = "binomial")

Now, Ill compare the performance of the six models:

Conclusions:

There is not enough information to build a reliable model, given that the use of all variables maxed out reliability at just above 60%. The best indicator seemed to be the rank of the school one went to for their undergraduate in combination with both a higher gre score and a higher gpa.
